Polygon Animals is built around conservation. Every species in the experience is a real animal that faces real threats in Thai waters today. The low-poly aesthetic is intentional: just as the geometry of each creature is constructed from a finite number of fragile triangles, the ecosystems that support these animals can fracture when even a single element is removed.

<Note>
  All species featured in Polygon Animals are either protected wildlife under Thailand's Wildlife Conservation and Protection Act or are listed on the IUCN Red List. Several appear on CITES Appendix I, which prohibits all international trade.
</Note>

## Why polygon art?

Each animal in Polygon Animals is composed of hundreds of geometric shards — triangles of color assembled into a recognizable form. Remove enough triangles and the shape collapses. The metaphor mirrors conservation biology: ecosystems are assemblages of interdependent parts, and biodiversity loss is rarely the removal of a single piece in isolation. The polygon aesthetic makes fragility visible.
